Privacy Policy

Move2Swipe  ·  Last updated: May 19, 2026

Move2Swipe ("the App") is developed and operated by The Chaos Group Consulting, LLC ("we", "us", or "our"). This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding that information.

1. Information We Collect

a. Account Information

If you choose to sign in with Google, we collect your Google account email address and a unique account identifier. Sign-in is optional — the App works fully without an account.

b. Health & Fitness Data

With your explicit permission, the App reads fitness data from Android Health Connect, including:

  • Step count
  • Distance traveled
  • Exercise session duration
  • Active calories burned

This data is read locally on your device to verify activity completions. It is never transmitted to our servers or shared with third parties.

c. App Usage & Activity Data

With your permission, the App reads which apps are in the foreground using Android's Usage Stats API. This data is used solely to enforce the app-blocking feature and is never transmitted off your device.

d. Habit & Preference Data

If you sign in, the following data is synced to our cloud database (Google Firestore) to allow backup and restore across devices:

  • Your configured habits and completion history (last 90 days)
  • Your list of blocked apps
  • App preferences (day shape, lifestyle goals, notification settings, etc.)
  • Streak milestone records

This data is associated with your Google account ID and stored securely in Google Firestore.

e. Purchase Information

Subscription and purchase status is managed entirely by Google Play. We do not collect or store payment card details. We receive only a non-personally-identifiable token confirming your subscription status.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To operate core app features (activity verification, app blocking, time bank)
  • To back up and restore your data when you sign in across devices
  • To send local notifications about your time bank balance (no personal data leaves your device for this)
  • To process and verify your subscription status via Google Play

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics profiling, or any purpose beyond operating the App.

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties, except as necessary to operate the App:

  • Google Firebase / Firestore — cloud storage for signed-in users (Google's privacy policy applies)
  • Google Play Billing — subscription management (Google's privacy policy applies)
  • Android Health Connect — fitness data is read locally; Health Connect's own access controls apply

4. Data Retention

Cloud-synced data is retained as long as your account exists. Completion history older than 90 days is not synced. You can delete all cloud data by signing out and contacting us to request account deletion (see Section 7).

5. Children's Privacy

Move2Swipe is intended for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

6. Security

Your cloud data is stored in Google Firestore with authentication-gated access rules — only you can read or write your own data. Fitness and usage data never leaves your device. We follow industry-standard practices to protect any data we handle.

7. Your Rights & Data Deletion

You may:

  • Sign out at any time from Settings → Account, which stops cloud sync
  • Request deletion of all cloud data by emailing us at support@congrove.org with the subject "Data Deletion Request"
  • Revoke Health Connect permissions at any time in the Android Health Connect app
  • Revoke app usage access at any time in Android Settings → Special App Access

We will fulfill data deletion requests within 30 days.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
